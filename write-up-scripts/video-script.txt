OFWs are one of the Philippines’ and Southeast Asia’s biggest economic lifelines. Remittances bring in billions of dollars a year — one of the region’s largest sources of foreign currency.

But cross-border remittances are complicated. Layers of regulation. Infrastructure gaps. Middlemen at every step. And the recipients — usually the OFW’s spouse, parents, children, siblings, or even grandparents — are the ones who bear the cost of every fee and delay.

Now here comes Echo — your voice-first remittance app, powered by Morph.

These huge fees and clunky processes need to be optimized. Your grandma doesn’t need to see 12 seed key phrases. Just use an email to create a wallet. Send money to that email. And the best part? Attach a voice message with it.

“Nako, need na naman ng pang-baon ni bunso — send bunso 500 pesos worth of USDT every Monday until next month.”

Done. Live FX rates. No menus. No forms. Just your voice.

And here’s the power of Morph Blockchain — automate recurring payments, and stream payments by the second to multiple recipients using Rules.

And when mama needs some cash, just cash out to any e-wallet or bank to transfer any tokens.  

What’s next for Echo? Mainnet launch is on the horizon, with native iOS and Android apps rolling out soon — putting voice-first remittance directly in the pockets of millions of OFWs and their families.
